02

Productized fleet packages

Four named, sellable products — separate from the $219 retail "complete." Named packages make the sale concrete and let buyers pick a tier instead of negotiating from scratch.

Onyx Fleet — Maintenance

Recurring exterior wash, wheels, glass + quick interior wipe-down. Keeps branded trucks photo-ready. The core recurring product.

Best for: HVAC / plumbing / pest / service fleets · Weekly or bi-weekly

Onyx Fleet — Full Detail

Complete wash + full interior vacuum, wipe, glass. The step-up recurring tier.

Best for: rentals, black-car, shuttles · Bi-weekly or monthly

Onyx Recon-Ready

Per-unit deep detail — full interior & exterior, shampoo/extraction, dressing. Sale-prep quality.

Best for: dealers, auctions, RV/powersports · Per-unit, ongoing volume

Onyx Sanitize+

Documented disinfectant treatment added to any detail. Built for patient-transport compliance.

Best for: NEMT / medical transport · Per the contract schedule
Each package maps to one buyer — the fleet manager picks the one that fits, not a sprawling menu. (Same discipline applies to retail: keep it to two tiers, Express & Executive — see the Field notes. Too many options kills bookings.)

Pricing & the path to $8–12k/month

Fleet pricing trades a lower per-vehicle price for volume + a recurring schedule. The retail anchor ($219 complete) stays for consumers; fleet gets its own per-vehicle rates. Here's a concrete model for a ~$10k month — the midpoint of the target.

Revenue sourceWhat it looks likeMonthly
Recurring fleet base · ~5 accountsAvg ~$1,000/account — e.g. a 20-truck service fleet on bi-weekly Maintenance ($25 × 20 × 2)$5,000

The one lever: each new recurring fleet account ≈ +$1,000 MRR. So the entire 90-day game is: sign ~6 recurring accounts and let premium retail fill the open days. Hit the low end ($8k) with 4–5 accounts; stretch to $12k with 7–8 or a bigger anchor.

Know your floor before every quote

The floor formula

(products per vehicle) + (target hourly × hours per vehicle) + (drive ÷ vehicles in the stop) = cost floor. Quote above it, always. If a volume discount pushes near the floor, raise the cadence or trim the scope — don't work for free.

Real times to price against

Working detailers hit full detail < 3 hrs, express < 2 hrs, exterior + wheels ≤ 1.5 hrs — and protect a ~$100/hr floor. A "cheap" wash that eats 90 min loses money. Use these to size each package's per-vehicle time. (more in the Field notes)

The volume closer: offer 10% off the whole job when several vehicles are done same-day, same-location. It rewards exactly the density that makes fleet work profitable — and gives you a clean way to price a yard without discounting per truck.

Where Onyx wins

Attack the under-served lane

Most competitors chase retail + semi-trucks. The recurring small/medium fleet lane — HVAC, NEMT, dealer-recon clusters in the prospect list — is less saturated. Lead with the priority segments.

Out-credential a hose-and-bucket

Wash-water reclamation (stormwater compliance), proof of insurance for commercial lots, dependable scheduling, and a 5.0★ record — what a cheaper one-man-with-a-hose can't match on a commercial property.

Win on invoicing, not discounts

Large fleets often prefer clean monthly billing / net terms over a price cut. Professional invoicing can close the account.

Price with confidence

Detailing is not a taxable service in Texas (Comptroller 96-259) — no sales tax to add. Quote clean, mid-market numbers.

Sales assets & operations

Pipeline tracker

One sheet, 7 stages: Prospect → Contacted → Trial booked → Trial done → Proposed → Signed → Active. No lead dies without a hard no or 4+ touches.

Routing from 75216

Batch jobs by area/day (NW = Harry Hines, NE = Garland, near = Oak Cliff). A 20-truck Maintenance ≈ a half/full day.

Capacity & the hire trigger

Solo holds ~5–8 active recurring accounts + retail. When you're booked solid ~1 week out and still turning away same-day work, that's the signal to add a part-time tech or 2nd rig — not a flex to ride.

Commercial prerequisites (have these ready)

Dealer lots and fleets often require a certificate of insurance before you touch a vehicle — carry liability + garage-keeper coverage. And on a commercial lot you must reclaim your wash water (Dallas stormwater rule). Both are table stakes for B2B — and a wedge a hose-and-bucket can't clear. (rules + kit in the Texas & DFW guide)

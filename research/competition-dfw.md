## DFW pricing benchmarks (what the local market charges)

Use these to position Onyx's pricing — mid-market, not the bottom (see Field notes).

| Service | DFW range |
|---|---|
| Full detail (standard) | **$185–$330** |
| Full detail (premium) | **$399–$449+** |
| Interior-only | $110–$225 ($200–$280+ heavy) |
| Exterior-only | $75–$150 |
| Mobile (convenience premium) | $195–$300+ |
| Ceramic coating | high hundreds and up |

Operator entry points seen: Panda Hub express **$79.91** / full $227 / premium $272; LG Mobile full-interior from **$130**; My Detail Guys from **$139** (single) / **$199** (full).

## DFW competitors (and who already does fleet)

The competitive landscape — and proof the DFW *fleet* market is active. **Bold = explicitly runs fleet/commercial programs** (direct competition for Onyx's strategy).

- **[Drive-By Detailing](https://drivebydetailingtx.com/)** — "#1 mobile detailing in Dallas," **110+ 5-star reviews**, explicit **commercial fleet + semi-truck**, ceramic/correction. Strongest local fleet competitor.
- **[On The Spot Mobile Detail](https://www.mobiledetailing-dallas.com/)** — **fleet washes with named B2B clients: Carvana, Enjoy, Heart of Hospice, Intersection.** Confirms the dealer/recon angle in our Leads list is real and being served.
- **[Texas Elite Detailing](https://www.texaselitedetailingco.com/)** — premium mobile + **fleet** + ceramic, DFW.
- **[SHWASH](https://www.shwash.co/dallas-tx)** — DFW-wide mobile; **maintains fleet vehicles** for area businesses.
- **[Fast Lane Detailing Solutions](https://fastlanedetailingtexas.com/)** — **fleet maintenance, heavy-equipment, auction prep**, hail/dent, tint (Kennedale/FW).
- **[My Detail Guys](https://mydetailguys.com/)** — Fort Worth; **semi-truck / fleet** solutions.
- **[Tic Tac Mobile Wash & Detailing](https://tictacdetailing.com/)** — DFW; **fleet** detailing.

### How to frame this in the GTM (competitive positioning)
- **Pricing:** Onyx sits mid-market ($185–$330 full detail); don't undercut to the $79 express tier — race-to-the-bottom trap.
- **Differentiators a hose-and-bucket competitor can't match:** wash-water reclamation (TCEQ/Dallas stormwater compliance — see `texas-dfw.html`), proof of insurance for commercial lots, reliability/scheduling.
- **Gap to attack:** several competitors chase retail + semi trucks; the *recurring small/medium fleet* lane (HVAC, NEMT, dealer recon clusters) in our Leads list is less saturated. On The Spot already has Carvana — so the dealer-recon angle is proven *and* contested; lead with the segments in the [priority hit list](../leads/00-priority-hitlist.md).

## Smaller finds worth a line in GTM

- **Fleet-closing tactic:** large fleets sometimes prefer **monthly billing / net terms over a discount** — clean invoicing can win the account. ([Professional Carwashing](https://www.carwash.com/fleet-accounts-how-to-land-and-keep-them/))
- **TX contract help:** a Texas attorney's page on [legal contracts for detailing businesses in Texas](https://ronaldrohdelaw.com/legal-contracts-for-mobile-detailing-business-texas/) — for putting fleet accounts in writing.
- **Permit nuance:** one source says Dallas doesn't require a general business license for mobile car wash, but there is a [Dallas business registration portal](https://dallascrm.my.site.com/registerbusiness/s/) — verify.
- **Tax (verified):** detailing is **not** a taxable service in Texas ([Comptroller 96-259](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/publications/96-259.php)) — many blogs claim otherwise; they're wrong.

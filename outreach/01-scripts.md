# Scripts — call, text, email & walk-up

Copy-paste starting points. Swap in the business name and the role you're asking for. Keep it short — you're booking a **trial**, not closing on the first contact.

> Golden rule: **lead with the problem you solve, ask for the decision-maker, propose a trial.** Never open with price.

---

## 1. Cold call (the workhorse)

**Opening — get to the right person:**
> "Hi, this is Rob with Onyx Detail Collective. Who handles keeping your [trucks / vans / lot vehicles] clean — is that you or someone else?"

**Once you've got them:**
> "Great. I run a mobile detailing service — I come to your yard, so your vehicles never leave to get cleaned. I work with [HVAC companies / dealers / rental fleets] keeping their fleet looking sharp on a set schedule. I'd love to do **one truck as a no-pressure trial** so you can see the work. If you like it, we set up a schedule; if not, no harm. Could I swing by this week?"

**If "we already have someone":**
> "Totally fair. A lot of folks keep me as backup for when their guy's slammed or a vehicle needs a deeper clean fast. Mind if I do one as a trial so I'm on your radar?"

**If "send me info":**
> "Will do — what's the best email or cell? I'll send a one-pager and a couple before/afters, then follow up Friday." *(Then actually follow up Friday.)*

---

## 2. Opener variants by buyer type

**Image-driven** (service companies, exotic/black-car):
> "Your trucks are out in driveways all day — they're basically rolling billboards. I keep fleets like yours photo-ready so every job site sees a clean, professional brand."

**Volume/recon** (dealers, auctions, RV):
> "I add reconditioning capacity right on your lot — per-car, on your schedule, so units get sale-ready without clogging your shop."

**Compliance-driven** (NEMT / medical transport):
> "I run a recurring sanitize-and-detail program built for patient-transport vans — interiors disinfected and documented on a fixed schedule, no vehicle downtime since I come to you."

**Facilities/ops** (hotels, clubs, property mgmt):
> "I can be your single scheduled vendor for every vehicle on the property — shuttles, valet cars, maintenance trucks — all kept to your brand standard, on-site."

---

## 3. Text message (for owner-run small fleets)

> Hi [Name], this is Rob with Onyx Detail Collective — mobile fleet detailing here in Dallas. I come to your yard so your [trucks] never miss a day of work. Can I do one as a free-look trial this week? Happy to send a couple before/after pics.

---

## 4. Cold email

**Subject:** Keeping [Company]'s fleet sharp — on-site, on a schedule

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm Rob with **Onyx Detail Collective**, a mobile detailing service based in Dallas. I work with [HVAC and plumbing companies / dealerships / rental fleets] keeping their vehicles clean **without the vehicles ever leaving the yard** — I come to you, on a set schedule.
>
> For a fleet like yours, that means [trucks that always look professional in front of customers / units that get sale-ready faster / vans kept to sanitation standards].
>
> I'd like to **detail one vehicle as a trial** so you can see the work firsthand — no commitment. If it's a fit, we set up a weekly or monthly plan.
>
> Are you open to a quick call this week? I'm at [phone].
>
> Thanks,
> Rob — Onyx Detail Collective
> [phone] · [email] · [Instagram/site]

*Keep it under 120 words. Attach or link 2–3 before/after photos.*

---

## 5. Walk-up (dealer clusters & yards — your best channel)

For the [Harry Hines and Forest Lane lots](../leads/01-auto-dealers-auctions.md), walking in beats calling. Owners are on-site.

> "Hey, I'm Rob with Onyx Detail Collective — mobile detailing. I detail recon units right here on the lot so you don't tie up staff or send cars out. What do you usually pay per car to get one front-line ready? I'll beat the hassle, not necessarily the price — I come to you and I'm reliable. Want me to do one right now so you can see it?"

Bring: business cards, a few printed before/afters, and your kit so you can **do a car on the spot** if they say yes. An on-the-spot demo closes lots.

---

## 6. Follow-up cadence (where the money actually is)

Most fleet deals close on the 2nd–4th touch, not the first.

- **Touch 1:** call or walk-up → book trial or get email.
- **Touch 2 (48 hrs):** email/text the one-pager + before/afters.
- **Touch 3 (Fri):** "Still happy to do that trial — what day works?"
- **Touch 4 (next week):** quick value text — a tip, a slot opening, a seasonal angle ("pollen season's brutal on white trucks").
- Then monthly until a yes or a hard no.

Log every touch in your tracker (see the [signals file](03-signals-and-finding-leads.md) for a simple format).

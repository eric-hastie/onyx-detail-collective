# The Playbook — how to win fleet accounts

The whole strategy on one page. Read this once; the scripts and pricing files turn it into action.

## Why fleet work (vs. one-off retail)

Retail detailing is a treadmill — every dollar requires finding a new customer. **Fleet detailing is recurring revenue:** land an account once, service it on a schedule, get paid predictably. Ten retail cars = ten sales. One 30-truck HVAC fleet on a monthly plan = one sale, twelve months of work.

Your edge as a **mobile** operator: fleets usually **can't** bring vehicles to a shop (trucks are out earning, vans are too big for a wash bay, exotics can't sit at a car wash, NEMT vans can't take downtime). You come to their yard. That's the entire pitch.

## The four kinds of fleet buyer (and how each thinks)

| Buyer type | Examples | What they care about | Your angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Image-driven** | HVAC/plumbing/pest, exotic rental, black-car | The vehicle is a billboard or the product | "A clean fleet sells for you. Dirty trucks cost you jobs." |
| **Volume/recon** | Dealers, auctions, RV/powersports | Throughput — get units sale-ready fast | "I add recon capacity on your lot, per car, no bottleneck." |
| **Compliance-driven** | NEMT, medical transport | Sanitation is regulated, not optional | "Compliance-grade sanitize + detail on a fixed schedule." |
| **Facilities/ops** | Hotels, clubs, property mgmt, senior living | One less vendor headache, brand standard | "One scheduled vendor for every vehicle on the property." |

Match your opening line to the buyer type. The [scripts file](01-scripts.md) has versions for each.

## The model that wins: land a trial → convert to a contract

1. **Trial detail.** Offer to do **one or two vehicles** at a fair (not free) price so they can see your work. Removes all risk for them.
2. **Walk the result.** Show the owner/manager the finished vehicle in person. This is where the contract is sold — not on the phone.
3. **Propose a schedule.** Weekly / bi-weekly / monthly depending on fleet type (see [pricing](02-pricing.md)). Per-vehicle price × cadence = predictable monthly invoice.
4. **Lock it in writing.** A one-page agreement: scope, cadence, price, what counts as "extra." Protects both sides and makes you look professional.
5. **Over-deliver early, then ask for referrals.** Fleet owners know other fleet owners. One happy HVAC company introduces you to their plumber friend.

## Three rules that keep this working

1. **Talk to the right person.** At a dealer it's the *used-car / recon manager*, not sales. At a service company it's the *owner or fleet/facilities manager*. At a hotel it's the *chief engineer*. The scripts name the role for each.
2. **Never lead with price.** Lead with the problem you solve, then the trial. Price comes after they've seen the work.
3. **Be reliable before you're cheap.** Fleets fire detailers for no-shows, not for being $10 more. Show up on schedule, every time — reliability is the product you're really selling.

# Pricing Fleet Contracts

Fleet pricing is different from retail. Retail = max dollars per car. Fleet = **lower per-vehicle price in exchange for volume + recurring schedule + you don't have to re-sell every job.** The math works because your time-per-vehicle drops when they're lined up in one yard.

> These are **frameworks and ranges to start from**, not Rob's final numbers. Rob sets actual prices from his own costs (products, time, drive, target hourly). Use these to structure the *offer*, then plug in his real figures.

## The core idea: per-vehicle price × cadence = predictable monthly invoice

A 20-truck HVAC fleet on a bi-weekly exterior wash + monthly interior:
- 20 trucks × wash price × 2/month → exterior revenue
- 20 trucks × interior price × 1/month → interior revenue
- **= one recurring monthly invoice**, booked in advance, route-efficient (all at one yard).

## Tiered service menu (build the contract from these)

| Tier | What's included | Typical cadence | Per-vehicle starting range* |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Maintenance wash** | Exterior wash, wheels, windows, quick wipe-down | Weekly / bi-weekly | $15–$35 |
| **Standard fleet detail** | Wash + full interior vacuum, wipe, glass | Bi-weekly / monthly | $40–$90 |
| **Deep / recon detail** | Full interior + exterior, shampoo/extraction, dressing | Monthly / per-unit (dealers) | $90–$200+ |
| **Sanitize add-on (NEMT)** | Disinfectant treatment, documented | Per the contract's schedule | $20–$50 add-on |

\* *Starting ranges for structuring the offer only — these are not quotes. Lower end = high-volume single-yard; higher end = larger vehicles, deeper work, or fewer units.*

## Volume & cadence discounts (how to make the recurring ask)

- **Volume:** the more vehicles at one stop, the lower the per-vehicle price — your drive time amortizes across the fleet. Build a 2–3 tier break (e.g. 1–9 / 10–24 / 25+ vehicles).
- **Commitment:** offer a modest discount for a signed 3–6 month schedule vs. ad-hoc. Predictability is worth a few dollars off.
- **Bundle:** "exterior every visit, interior every other visit" keeps each visit fast and the price steady.

## Pricing by buyer type

- **Service fleets (HVAC/plumbing/pest):** price the **maintenance wash** low to win the recurring route; upsell periodic standard details. Win on cadence, not margin-per-truck.
- **Dealers / auctions / RV:** price **per unit, recon tier.** They already have a number in mind ("we pay $X to get one front-line ready") — ask what it is, then match the convenience of on-lot service.
- **Exotic / black-car:** price **higher per vehicle** — these owners pay for white-glove care and the cars demand it. Don't underprice premium work.
- **NEMT / medical:** standard detail + **sanitize add-on**; the compliance angle justifies a premium and a strict cadence.
- **Property mgmt / hotels:** bundle everything on the property into one monthly number; they value "one vendor, one invoice."

## How to present it (don't hand over a rate sheet cold)

1. **Do the trial first.** Let the work justify the price.
2. **Quote the schedule, not the car.** "I can keep all 20 trucks sharp for **$X/month**, every other Tuesday" lands better than "$25/truck."
3. **Anchor on their cost of *not* doing it** — lost brand impression, slower recon, failed sanitation check.
4. **Put it in a one-page agreement:** scope, cadence, price, what's "extra" (excessive mud, pet hair, biohazard), payment terms (Net 15/30 or on-visit), and a simple 30-day cancel clause.

## Quick cost-floor check (so Rob never prices below profit)

Before quoting, know your floor per vehicle:

> **(products per vehicle) + (your target hourly × hours per vehicle) + (drive/setup allocated across the fleet) = cost floor.**
> Quote above the floor. If the volume discount pushes you near the floor, raise the cadence or trim the scope — don't work for free.
